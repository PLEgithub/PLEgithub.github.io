404Oops, nothing here!

We can't find the page you're looking for... but that's alright:

Sometimes, users are directed to to pages on your site that don’t exist. This could happen for number of reasons - maybe they tried to access an older link that doesn’t work, or tried to manually type in a direct URL that never existed in the first place. The point is, sometimes users on your site will get errors.

But from an SEO perspective, this is still an opportunity: rather than present users with an ugly, standard error page they’re used to when they try to break the Internet, why not instead spend a few moments to design a custom 404 error page that will boost interaction with your site?

Some benefits include:

  • Offers a chance at a consistent site design, along with full navigation options to other, working parts of the site, or even suggested replacement destination pages within the site
  • Creates a more seamless user experience while on the site; there’s no disruption with a jarring black-and-white HTML error
  • By keeping people from backing out of the site (especially if the 404 was served from a search engine) custom 404 pages help lower bounce rate, increase time spent on-site, and offers users a chance to keep browsing content and interacting with the site/brand

Tips for building custom 404 pages:

  • Offering a humorous 404 experience can add a little humanity to the user experience, however be careful not to do anything that would confuse the user… you want them happy and clicking
  • If being used generally, offer links to your most popular articles/posts/sections on your 404
  • Ability to offer users a way to report dead or broken links to be updated
  • More advanced developers can use the information passed through 404 as to what type of broken link the user was trying to access (broken link from search engine, from within the site, manually typed in, etc.) to offer different custom 404 experiences based on the type of error (more info linked below)
